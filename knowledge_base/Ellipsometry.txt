TOOL: Spectroscopic Ellipsometry (SE)

WHAT IT MEASURES:
- Thin film thickness (single layer or multilayer stacks) from ~0.1 nm to several microns
- Optical constants: refractive index (n) and extinction coefficient (k) as a function of wavelength
- Film composition, porosity, and surface roughness via optical modeling

RESOLUTION: Thickness sensitivity: 0.01–0.1 nm; lateral resolution: ~50 µm (standard); ~1 µm (imaging ellipsometry)

BEST FOR:
- Non-destructive, non-contact measurement of transparent or semi-transparent thin film thickness
- Monitoring film growth in real-time (in-situ ellipsometry on deposition chambers)
- Characterizing optical properties of dielectric, semiconductor, and organic thin films

NOT SUITABLE FOR:
- Opaque metallic films thicker than ~50–100 nm (limited light penetration)
- Samples with significant surface roughness or non-uniform films without careful modeling

SAMPLE PREP:
No sample preparation required for most solid flat substrates — one of the most non-invasive techniques available. Samples should be clean and free of surface contamination. Films measured on reflective substrates (silicon, glass, metals) yield the best signal-to-noise. Liquids and soft films can be measured in liquid cells.

OUTPUT:
Measured ellipsometric angles Ψ (psi) and Δ (delta) vs. wavelength; fitted thickness values from optical models (Cauchy, Sellmeier, Tauc-Lorentz); n and k dispersion curves; goodness-of-fit parameter (MSE); in real-time mode, thickness vs. time traces during deposition.

COST/AVAILABILITY:
$20–$80/hr at shared facilities; instruments cost $50K–$300K. Common at semiconductor and photonics research labs, thin film deposition facilities, and university materials departments. Often integrated directly into deposition systems for in-situ monitoring.
