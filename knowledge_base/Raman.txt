TOOL: Raman Spectroscopy

WHAT IT MEASURES:
- Molecular bond vibrations, chemical structure, and phase identification
- Stress/strain in crystalline materials via peak shift analysis
- Layer number, defect density, and doping in 2D materials (graphene, MoS2, etc.)

RESOLUTION: Lateral spatial resolution: ~0.5–1 µm (diffraction-limited); depth: 1–10 µm (material-dependent); spectral resolution: 0.5–2 cm-1

BEST FOR:
- Non-destructive chemical fingerprinting of organic, inorganic, and carbon-based materials
- Characterizing graphene, carbon nanotubes, and 2D materials (G band, 2D band, D band)
- Identifying polymorphs, phase transitions, and crystallinity in pharmaceutical or ceramic samples

NOT SUITABLE FOR:
- Metals and highly reflective surfaces (strong background fluorescence or poor Raman signal)
- Elemental composition quantification — Raman identifies bonds and phases, not absolute elemental content

SAMPLE PREP:
Minimal preparation required — one of the most convenient characterization techniques. Samples can be solid, powder, liquid, or thin film. Samples should be clean and free of fluorescent contaminants. Powders are pressed onto a glass slide or placed in a sealed vial; thin films are measured directly on their substrate.

OUTPUT:
Raman spectra (intensity vs. Raman shift in cm-1); peak position, width (FWHM), and relative intensity data; spatial maps of peak intensity or position across a sample area (Raman mapping mode); stress/strain maps derived from peak shift.

COST/AVAILABILITY:
$30–$120/hr at shared facilities; benchtop systems cost $30K–$200K. Very widely available at universities and industrial labs. Portable and handheld Raman systems also available for field use (~$10K–$50K).
